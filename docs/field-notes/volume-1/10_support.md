If this volume was useful — if it changed how you think about an open
psionic platform, or saved you an evening of digging through the wiki,
or just earned a place on the small shelf of documents you trust — here
is how to support the work that produced it.

There are four doors, ordered by commitment. Anything in the first three
is welcomed and meaningful at any level.

## 1. Star the repo

[github.com/Jthora/HelmKit](https://github.com/Jthora/HelmKit). Costs
nothing. Helps the project surface to the people it is for, through
GitHub's recommendation and trending mechanisms. The single highest-
leverage action a reader who *liked the volume but doesn't want to spend
money* can take.

## 2. Tip the work

[ko-fi.com/helmkit](https://ko-fi.com/helmkit) accepts one-off tips at
any amount. Tips fund parts, firmware compute time, and — most
importantly — the hours not sold to other clients. The project's
discipline runs on those hours. Field Notes Vol. I exists because of
roughly forty of them.

## 3. Join the interest list

The Tier 1 DIY kit and Tier 2 hand-built unit are not yet open for
pre-order. The
[interest list](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLScESYAx0iFJynlNEPns250Foy3LRBQ6G8HrXOtipnrbTl9CBw/viewform?usp=header)
is the channel through which their availability will be announced. Zero
cost, zero commitment, and the list is not used for any other purpose.

## 4. Commission a custom build

Custom HelmKit commissions are open at $5,000 and up, with an 8 to 12
week lead time and a 50% non-refundable deposit. A commission is built
to the customer's spec on the open HelmKit platform — a specific
hardpoint loadout, a specific firmware preset list, a specific frame
finish — and ships with a copy of this volume plus the customer's
device documentation as a standalone PDF. Commissions are the only
revenue stream that funds new module development at present.

Inquire through the interest form above or open a GitHub issue tagged
`commission-inquiry`.

## What is currently on Ko-fi

- **Field Notes Vol. I (digital PDF).** $9 base price, pay-what-you-want
  above that. Ships immediately as a download.
- **Tier 1 DIY Build Kit (digital).** $35. Files, BOM, build guide,
  firmware images. Ships when the Mk1 design freezes; current target
  is the end of 2026 Q3.
- **Tier 2 Hand-Built Unit.** $500 deposit, balance on shipment. Ships
  in waves; current target is 2026 Q3-Q4 for the first wave.
- **Custom Commission.** $5,000+, see above.

The price points are deliberately layered. The PDF makes the project
readable to anyone who can spend $9. The DIY kit makes the project
buildable to anyone with a 3D printer and a soldering iron. The hand-
built unit makes the project ownable without those skills. The custom
commission makes the project a vehicle for someone else's specific
research question. All four roads land on the same open platform; the
files behind the DIY kit are the same files the hand-built unit ships
with.

## What this is not

This is **not a crowdfunding campaign**. There is no goal bar, no
all-or-nothing threshold, no stretch tier promising features the
project has not committed to in its roadmap. The roadmap (Chapter 7)
is the project's commitment device, and tips do not buy roadmap
position. They buy hours; the hours work the roadmap as it stands.

This is also **not a recurring subscription** in any form. The project
does not run on Patreon, does not have monthly tier benefits, and does
not gate documentation behind a paywall. The full repository — every
chapter source, every CAD file, every notebook, every BOM — is open
and stays open. Field Notes Vol. I has a $9 price tag because the
*assembly* of it into a printable, indexable volume took dedicated time;
the underlying material is at
[github.com/Jthora/HelmKit](https://github.com/Jthora/HelmKit) and
costs nothing to read.

## Reproducibility

Every numerical figure in this volume regenerates from a script under
[`notebooks/`](https://github.com/Jthora/HelmKit/tree/master/notebooks) in
the project repository. Every derivation is owned end-to-end by the
project and ships with its source under
[`docs/derivations/`](https://github.com/Jthora/HelmKit/tree/master/docs/derivations).
The build script that produced this PDF lives at
[`docs/field-notes/volume-1/build.sh`](https://github.com/Jthora/HelmKit/blob/master/docs/field-notes/volume-1/build.sh)
and uses pandoc plus tectonic with the LaTeX template under
[`docs/field-notes/volume-1/template.tex`](https://github.com/Jthora/HelmKit/blob/master/docs/field-notes/volume-1/template.tex).
A reader who clones the repository at the same git tag this volume was
built at can regenerate this PDF byte-for-byte modulo timestamps.

## How to read this volume

The chapters are written to be read in order on a first pass and used
as reference on subsequent passes. Chapter 1 (Manifesto) is the
project's epistemic stance. Chapter 2 (Platform) is the hardware
architecture overview. Chapter 3 (Sister modules) places the HelmKit
inside its broader project family. Chapter 4 (Wiki as specification) is
the project's reading protocol for the FusionGirl wiki and its
claim-language firewall. Chapter 5 (Field theory) is the first batch of
project-owned derivations against the wiki's geometries. Chapter 6
(Safety floor) is binding on every wearer, operator, and contributor.
Chapter 7 (Roadmap) is the seven-step Mk ladder and the gating logic
between rungs. Chapter 8 (Field notes) is the bench-session log to
date. Chapter 9 (Prior art) is the annotated literature this project
engages with. Chapter 10 (Support the work) is how to get involved.

This is **Volume I**. It corresponds to the Mk0 + Mk0.5 + Mk1 design
window. Subsequent volumes will cover later Mk-generations as they
land. Each volume is intended to be a self-contained snapshot rather
than a serialized continuation.

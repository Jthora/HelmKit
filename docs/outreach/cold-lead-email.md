# Outreach email — re-engagement (cold-lead variant)

_Use this template when reaching out to people who previously expressed interest in HelmKit™ but whom you have not actively maintained contact with. Treat as a re-introduction, not a follow-up. Edit for voice and personalization on every send._

---

## Subject line — pick one

- `HelmKit is real now — here's where it stands`
- `The helmet, an update — and three ways in`
- `Briefly: HelmKit is shipping artifacts, not just ideas`

(My pick: the first. Direct, concrete, no hype.)

---

## Send guidance

- **Personalize the first paragraph for each recipient.** Reference what they specifically said when they reached out, or the channel they reached out on. The whole point of the cold-lead variant is that even when you can't remember the conversation, you can at least name it.
- **Send one at a time, manually.** Mail-merge feel is the kiss of death for an audience this small. Each one takes 2–4 minutes; total send for ~10 leads is half an hour.
- **Subject line is the entire first impression.** Don't soften it. People who hear "I'm working on something" 50 times a year skip those. People who hear "this is real now and here's where it stands" open it.
- **Plain text email, not HTML.** Looks more personal, lands in fewer spam folders.
- **Send from your real email.** Not from a mailing-list platform. This is a personal note, not a campaign.

```
Interest:    [interest form link]
Support:     ko-fi.com/helmkit
Commission:  reply here

```

## After-send tracking

Keep a simple list (sheet or note) per recipient:

| Name | Channel | Sent date | Response | Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ... | email | 2026-05-17 | — | — |

If no response after **2 weeks**, send one short follow-up: "Hey, didn't want this to fall through the cracks — three doors are still open, in case you want them." No third nudge.
